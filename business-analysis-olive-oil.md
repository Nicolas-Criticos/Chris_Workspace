## The Numbers: Honest Assessment

✅ **Good News:**
- 34–40% gross margin is **solid for DTC food**
- Procurement at 5% is reasonable
- Your COGS (oil + tin + sticker = R881.60) is only 49.5% of selling price — healthy
- Single-bottle orders are still profitable (~R710 net)

⚠️ **Challenges:**
- **Delivery is your margin killer.** R100–R300 on a R1,780 sale eats 5.6–16.9% of gross profit
- **Procurement at 5%** is fair, but add logistics complexity
- **Sticker cost R5** is tiny (0.3% of price) — not worth optimizing
- **Volume needed:** To hit R50k/month revenue, you need ~28 bottles/month (2–3/day). Doable but needs consistent marketing

## Scaling Opportunities (In Order of Impact)

### 1. **Delivery Pooling (Biggest Lever)**
**Problem:** R200 avg delivery per order tanks margins on singles.  
**Solution:** Bundle orders by region/timing.

- **Strategy:** Weekly collection window ("Orders placed Mon–Wed ship Friday")
- **Result:** 4–5 orders per delivery run = R40–50 per bottle instead of R200
- **Implementation:** Use WhatsApp to manage cutoff times + announce shipping day

**Impact:** Save R150–200/order on bulk shipments = +11–14% margin lift.

### 2. **Offer Subscription / Recurring Orders**
- Customer subscribes to 1 bottle/month, auto-ships
- You batch these together, negotiate better delivery rates
- **Margin gain:** Predictable volume = better procurement pricing (maybe negotiate 4–6% instead of 5%)
- **Retention:** Subscriptions = repeat revenue, lower churn

**Example:** R1,780/month × 12 months = guaranteed income, better pricing from procurement company.

### 3. **Fractional Bottles (1L, 2.5L)**
- **1L bottles:** Higher margin % (45–50%), attract price-sensitive customers, smaller delivery footprint
- **Mixed orders:** Someone buys 1×5L + 2×1L = same delivery cost, higher total margin
- **Tin cost concern:** You'll need smaller tins. Investigate bulk pricing on 1L tins.

**Estimate:** 1L tins might drop from R51.60 to R15–20 at volume (1000+ units).

### 4. **Regional Hubs / Pick-up Model**
- Partner with local gift shops, health stores, or farmers markets in Cape Town, Johannesburg
- Customers collect locally, you ship in bulk to hub
- **Margin gain:** Eliminates last-mile delivery for some orders

**Cost:** Hub commission (5–10%) but offset by bulk delivery savings.

### 5. **Procurement Company Negotiation**
- Current: 5% of order value = R89/bottle
- Ask for: Tiered pricing (3% if >500 bottles/month, 2% if >1000/month)
- Ask about: Consolidated shipments, weekly instead of per-order

**Potential save:** 1–2% margin per bottle if you hit volume targets.

### 6. **Instagram Micro-Influencer Seeding**
- Send free bottles to micro-influencers in wellness/organic/sustainable niches (10k–100k followers)
- Cost: 2–3 bottles/month = R3,560 cost, but audience reach = R500k+ impressions
- **Result:** Lower CAC (customer acquisition cost) than paid ads, organic word-of-mouth

**ROI:** If it converts 1% = 5000 impressions → 50 clicks → 5 orders = R3,545 revenue. Break-even easily, with brand lift.

## Honest Bottom Line

Your margins are **healthy for DTC (34–40%)**, but your business is **delivery-constrained**. You're not losing money; you're leaving money on the table by shipping singles at R200 each.
